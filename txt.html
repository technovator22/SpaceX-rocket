Falcon 1

SpaceX's first launch vehicle, now retired. Falcon 1 was SpaceX's first rocket and a pivotal achievement in commercial spaceflight history. A two-stage rocket designed to carry small payloads to orbit, it became the first privately developed liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit in 2008. Its maiden flight was on March 24, 2006, but it wasn't until its fourth attempt that it successfully reached orbit. This milestone demonstrated the viability of SpaceX's innovative, cost-effective approach to space exploration. Falcon Heavy

Essentially three Falcon 9 rockets strapped together, it's the most powerful operational rocket in the world by a factor of two. First launched in February 2018, it features three Falcon 9 cores strapped together, giving it extraordinary thrust. With the capability to lift over 63,800 kg to Low Earth Orbit, Falcon Heavy is designed for missions carrying large payloads, including interplanetary journeys. Its reusability further reduces launch costs, with all three boosters capable of landing back on Earth. Dragon

A spacecraft used for cargo and crew missions to the International Space Station (ISS).Dragon, SpaceX's pioneering spacecraft, is designed for cargo and crew missions to the International Space Station (ISS). First launched in December 2010, Dragon became the first commercial spacecraft to deliver cargo to the ISS in 2012. The crewed version, Crew Dragon, debuted in May 2020 with the Demo-2 mission, safely transporting astronauts to and from the ISS. Dragon is known for its reusability and versatility, featuring advanced safety systems and the capability to carry up to seven astronauts.
